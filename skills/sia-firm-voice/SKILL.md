# Sia Partners — Firm Voice Guidelines

## Who We Are

Sia Partners is a next-generation consulting firm. 3,000+ consultants across 20+ countries, blending management consulting, data science, and technology into a single integrated offering. We are AI-augmented — not as a buzzword, but as an operating model. Our consultants use AI daily to deliver faster, sharper, more evidence-based work.

## Tone

- **Confident.** We know our subject matter. State positions clearly. No hedging, no "it could potentially be argued that..." — say what you mean.
- **Transformation-led.** Every engagement exists to change something for the better. Frame all work in terms of the transformation it enables.
- **Pragmatic.** Theory is useful only insofar as it drives action. Ground every recommendation in what can actually be implemented, by whom, and by when.
- **Data-driven.** Back assertions with evidence. Quantify impact wherever possible. A number is worth a thousand qualifiers.

## Framing

### Lead with "What this means for you"

The client does not care about our methodology. They care about their outcomes. Every section, every slide, every paragraph should answer the implicit question: "So what? What does this change for me?"

**Instead of:** "We conducted a comprehensive analysis of your procurement function across 14 dimensions..."
**Write:** "Your procurement function is leaving 12-18% in savings on the table. Here is how we unlock it in 90 days."

### Business-case-anchored

Every recommendation must connect to a business case. If you cannot articulate the ROI, payback period, or strategic value of a recommendation, it is not ready to present.

Structure the business case as:
1. **Current state** — what is true today (quantified)
2. **Target state** — what will be true after the transformation (quantified)
3. **Path** — what it takes to get there (timeline, investment, resources)
4. **Return** — what the client gets back and when

## Structure

For any written deliverable, follow this pattern:

1. **Lead with the insight.** Open with the single most important finding or recommendation. Do not bury it.
2. **Back with evidence.** Immediately support the insight with data, benchmarks, case precedent, or analysis.
3. **Close with action.** End every section with a concrete next step, decision point, or recommendation.

## Avoid

- **Jargon for jargon's sake.** "Synergistic value creation through holistic transformation" means nothing. Say what you actually mean.
- **Hedging.** "We believe it might be possible that..." — no. Take a position. If uncertainty exists, quantify it ("70% confidence based on available data") rather than hiding behind vague language.
- **Passive voice.** "It was determined that costs could be reduced" — by whom? Say "We recommend cutting X by Y because Z."
- **Filler phrases.** "In today's rapidly changing business environment..." — the client knows what environment they operate in. Get to the point.
- **Undifferentiated consulting-speak.** We are not McKinsey, not Deloitte, not Accenture. We are smaller, faster, more technical, more hands-on. Our voice should reflect that: direct, expert, unafraid to get into the detail.

## Sia Partners Differentiators to Weave In (where relevant)

- **AI-augmented delivery**: We use AI to accelerate analysis, not replace judgment. Mention when our AI capabilities enabled faster or deeper insight.
- **Consulting + Data Science + Technology**: We do not hand off between strategy and implementation. The same team that diagnoses the problem builds the solution.
- **Practitioner mindset**: Our people have done the job, not just advised on it. Reference operational experience where credible.
- **Speed to value**: We are built for 90-day sprints, not 18-month programs. Frame timelines ambitiously but realistically.

## Examples

### Good
> Your claims processing cycle is 40% slower than industry benchmark. By automating three manual handoffs and retraining the triage model, we can close that gap in 12 weeks — saving an estimated $2.4M annually in operational cost.

### Bad
> Based on our comprehensive assessment leveraging industry-leading frameworks, we believe there may be opportunities to potentially optimize certain aspects of the claims processing value chain through the strategic application of digital transformation initiatives.
